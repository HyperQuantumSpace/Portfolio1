AI Risk Management — NIST AI RMF 1.0 · Embedded Capability
⚛️ NIST AI RMF — CISO CAPABILITY INTEGRATION
The NIST AI RMF 1.0 (January 2023) is one of the frameworks the CISO employs to govern the enterprise AI risk surface. Spanning four functions — GOVERN, MAP, MEASURE, and MANAGE — it is embedded within the AI Security domain and cross-referenced across GRC, data security, TPRM, SOC operations, and executive reporting. It aligns with EU AI Act obligations, CISA AI security guidance, Executive Order 14110, and DoD AI ethics principles.
GOVERN MAP MEASURE MANAGE EU AI Act EO 14110 CISA AI Guidance DoD AI Ethics
Function 01
GOVERN
Culture, Policy & Accountability
  • Establish AI governance structure — executive AI risk committee, CISO as AI risk owner
  • Develop AI Risk Policy, AI Use Policy, and Acceptable Use standards enterprise-wide
  • Define roles: AI Risk Officer, AI Red Team, AI Compliance Lead (RACI matrix)
  • Integrate AI risk into enterprise risk register (ERM) with financial quantification
  • Build AI ethics principles aligned to NIST, OECD AI Principles, and DoD AI Ethics
  • Ensure GOVERN function is active before AI system design or procurement begins
  • Tie AI risk appetite to Board-level risk tolerance statements
GV-1 Policy GV-2 Culture GV-3 Roles GV-4 Org Teams GV-5 Policies GV-6 Policies
Function 02
MAP
Context, Risk ID & Classification
  • Maintain an AI system inventory — catalog all AI/ML models in production, dev, and procurement
  • Classify AI systems by risk tier: minimal, limited, high-risk, unacceptable (aligned to EU AI Act)
  • Map AI use cases to impacted stakeholders: employees, customers, regulators, partners
  • Conduct AI threat modeling: adversarial ML, data poisoning, model inversion, prompt injection
  • Identify sociotechnical risks: bias, fairness failures, explainability gaps, over-reliance
  • Assess third-party AI (SaaS, vendor models) within TPRM context
  • Document data lineage, training data sources, and known data quality issues
MP-1 Context MP-2 Use-Case MP-3 AI Impact MP-4 Risk IDs MP-5 Impacts
Function 03
MEASURE
Assessment, Testing & Metrics
  • Define AI risk metrics: model drift rates, adversarial robustness scores, fairness KPIs
  • Conduct pre-deployment AI red teaming: prompt injection, jailbreaks, data extraction
  • Run bias audits and fairness assessments across protected classes and demographics
  • Implement continuous AI monitoring — model performance, anomaly detection in outputs
  • Score AI systems on NIST trustworthy AI properties (accuracy, reliability, transparency)
  • Perform TEVV: Testing, Evaluation, Verification & Validation before production release
  • Benchmark AI system performance against defined organizational tolerances
MS-1 Measure MS-2 AI Red Team MS-3 Bias Audit MS-4 TEVV MS-5 Monitor
Function 04
MANAGE
Response, Treatment & Recovery
  • Develop AI Incident Response playbooks: model failure, adversarial attack, bias harm event
  • Implement risk treatment: accept, mitigate, transfer, or avoid AI-specific risks
  • Maintain AI system decommission and rollback procedures — kill switch protocols
  • Govern AI change management — model updates, retraining cycles, version control
  • Manage AI residual risk: document accepted risks with formal sign-off by risk owner
  • Track remediation status of AI risk findings; report to CISO dashboard and Board
  • Integrate AI incidents into enterprise SIEM/SOAR for correlation and response automation
MG-1 Prioritize MG-2 Respond MG-3 Correct MG-4 Monitor
